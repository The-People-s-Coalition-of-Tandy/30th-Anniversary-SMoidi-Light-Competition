RULES (=^-ω-^=) GUIDELINES
  1. Submit a properly formatted General MIDI composition (.MID) to our Submission Form BY March 21st ((((2 weeks after the halfway-point between the birthdays of MIDI co-founders Ikutaro Kakehashi *of ROLAND* and Dave Smith *of SEQUENTIAL CIRCUITS*)))).
  2. Audio files will NOT BE ACCEPTED! All submissions will recorded using the People's Coalition of Tandy's proprietary Midi hardware and sent back to contestants in high-resolution, lossless audio.
  3. All entries MUST be submitted in the form of a MIDI file (.mid) that abides to the General MIDI standard.
    1. ◊ Engraving software such as Sibelius, MuseScore, Finale, and Dorico are all General MIDI capable and can seamlessly format arrangements into General MIDI.
    2. ◊ DAWs such as Reaper, Ableton, Cubase, Logic, and Pro Tools can also generate General MIDI files, but Program Changes (ie. instrument selections) must be inputted manually. Certain DAWs may not be GM-compliant in their playback system, but that should not be a problem as long as you translate your program changes to their corresponding GM instruments. For those, there are many GM Soundfonts available which you can load into a sampler or sfz player!
    3. ◊ Here is a chart of all General Midi instruments and their corresponding Program Changes.
    4. ◊ Make sure you don't use audio plugins or outboard effects! The only acceptable effects will be the General MIDI standard Reverb and Chorus sends (effects 1 and 2; CC #91 and #93)!
  4. 2.5 minutes or less! Our judges don’t got all day! And include a title!
  5. Feel free to email any questions to pcotandy@gmail.com or post them to our Facebook event page
  6. The overall winner of the competition will receive an modest cash prize and the official title of "The People’s Coalition of TANDY GM General Midi, 30th Anniversary Warrior of the 30 SMoidi Light 30, ZRainbow Spectrume 2022 300 Award"! Award category winners will receive prestigious bespoke titles for professional and personal use.
